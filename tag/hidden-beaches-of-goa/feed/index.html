Places to visit while on a trip to Goa:

Chapora beach

Chapora Beach is a famous tourist destination in Goa. It is close to the Chapora Fort. This beach has white sand, black lava rocks, and groovy coconut trees. Isn’t it picture-perfect? And it is also famous for adventure sports. Chapora is just a few minutes away from the fishing jetty.

Palolem beach

Palolem beach is an uncrowded beach with white sand facing a blue bay. Canacona Island and Galgibaga Beach are just around the corner from here. It is famous for its silent disco parties, restaurants, and water sports. This is one of the most breathtaking and gorgeous beaches in South Goa.

Grande Island

Grande Island or Bat Island is another famous spot in Goa that you must visit. It has a tranquil environment that will provide you with a breathtaking experience. Grande Island is the best hub for scuba diving and snorkeling in Goa.

Panaji 

The largest and main city of Goa is Panaji. It is famous for its churches, historical buildings, monumental structures, and pristine beaches. Panaji is the most famous destination in Goa. It has been also featured in several Bollywood movies. Church Square is a popular spot to visit while in this city. This city has some Portuguese Baroque-styled, beautiful red-blue-green-yellow roofed buildings.

Old Goa

Old Goa is a stunning canvas. It features amazing architecture, colonial monuments, cobbled pathways, and impressive coastal scenery. Old Goa will definitely fascinate history buffs. It provides you with a glimpse of old Portugual culture and architecture. Old Goa is also a hub for many monuments and tourist spots.

Basilica of Bomb Jesus

This Jesuit church is India’s first Basilica. It is the finest example of the Baroque Style and Portuguese architecture. It is one of the most iconic monuments in Goa. UNESCO has termed it a World Heritage Site.

Hidden Beaches of Goa

Agonda Beaches

Agonda beach is located in Canacona city of South Goa. It is famous for its tranquil environment and alluring view. This place is one of the four beaches designated by the government as a turtle nesting site.

Agonda beach is also popular for water sports, nightlife, and shopping. Nightlife is quite exciting here and has some of the best Silent clubs. Restaurants, Cafes, and Bars add more colors to the nightlife.

Betalbatim Beach

Betabatim beach enjoys an incredible view of sunset and sunrise.  It is also known as Sunset Beach of Goa. With pine trees along the shoreline, it is a picturesque beach. One of the specialties of this beach is that it is also known as ‘lovers beach’. Love birds flock along the shoreline and enjoy the open skies of Goa. 

Betul Beach

It is situated near the sal river in South Goa. This gorgeous beach is ideal for fishing and strolling. If you want to have a quiet and peaceful time, you can go and chill at this location.

Butterfly Beach

Located in North Goa, Butterfly beach is a boat ride from Palolem beach.  You can experience a mystical sunset view at Butterfly beach. This unexploited beach houses colorful butterflies. It has a breathtaking panoramic view of the blue waters. This beach is also famous for Dolphine sightseeing.

Galgibaga Beach

Galgibaga beach is one of the best beaches in Goa with a silver sand shore and clear water. It houses coconut trees and pine trees that will amuse the photographer in you. Galgibaga beach is a serene nesting place for olive ridley turtles. This shore is home to peace-adoring fisherfolks.

Hollant Beach

Located in the foothills of the Western ghats, South Goa is best known for its splendid sunrise view. Hollant beach is also known as the land of serenity. It is a gorgeous landscape and an incredible beach for swimming. One can enjoy surfing near this beach.

Kalacha Beach

Kalacha Beach is well known for its sweet water lagoons. Located south of Querim beach, in South Goa, is Kalacha beach. It features green slopes that stretch all the way to the end of the shoreline.

Here, you may spot a lot of exotic birds. Nights may turn out to be a magical experience. Open-air and grooving music sessions will make it the time of your li.

Kokalem Beach

Kokalem beach is in South Goa. It is also known as Tiger beach. This least crowded and truly natural beach is where you can enjoy a solitary time.

Siridao Beach

Siridao Beach lies at the mouth of the Zuari river. This beach is known as ‘shell collectors beach’. It is a hub of unique shells, a wide range of oysters, and pearl shells.

Velsao Beach

Velsao beach is a calm beach with a picturesque landscape. Tourists can enjoy the mumbling of birds as it is great for birdwatching.

Vainguinim Beach

Vainguinim Beach is known for its silver sand seashore. It has greenish-blue water, exciting marine life, and an array of trees with birds. Vainguinim Beach offers a phenomenal experience to voyagers.

Best time to visit Goa

The best time to visit Goa is from Mid-November to Mid-February. These are the best month to visit Goa as the weather is pleasant, cool, and comfortable. This is the perfect time to relax on the beaches.

You will have a great time if you visit Goa in the month of December. Usually, it is brimming with tourists and amazing parties during this time. Christmas and New Year’s is the time when Goa is packed.

During monsoon, you will be able to enjoy Goa in the most tranquil way. This is the time when people avoid visiting Goa. During monsoons, you won’t be able to enjoy water sports. Goa is the perfect mix of natural beauty, revelry and tropical environs. But hasn’t it become a little too cliche? You don’t want to be stuck visiting the same churches and beaches, overflown with crowds. Well we are here to help you discover the unseen and underrated spots to eat, party and chill. Ukiyo stays brings you a guide for unique Goan experience, as witnessed by our travellers. Use this as a guide to know Goa inside out. 

 

It’s Beaching!

An average beach has everything from shacks and water sports to tattoo studios and eateries. It can be overwhelming to do it all in a day. But you don’t have to it all at the same place. So follow this guide to find beach specific activities!

 

 

i. Candolim Beach

 Candolim Beach is one of the quietest beaches where you can gaze upon the sparkling sea. Calamari is the place to be when you’re here. Try their scrumptious pastries in their aesthetic ambience. If you’re craving for authentic goan fish delicacies, drop by the fisherman’s nest. This is the ideal picture worthy spot where no crowds will disrupt your peace or photobomb. Eventual visits of locals with their pets completes the snuggie vibes.
 

ii. Baga Beach

 Baga is the most well-known beach here in Goa. This beach offers exotic food, plenty of activities and shacks in the glistening sand. Head to Britto’s to savour delicious cheesecakes. You can even impulsively get inked at Ink Craft Tattoos. The artist here has inked several celebrities and sportsmen. You can braid your hair by the beach or try water sports. Baga beach is the home to many small shops. Here you’ll find everything, from keepsakes to accessories. Close to the beach you can head to Baba Au Ram to vibe on some trance music. Get gifts and memoirs from Goa for all your loved ones! 
 

iii.  Morjim Beach

 Morjim is also one of the less crowded beaches in Goa. But there’s no reason to believe that you can’t have your share of water-based fun there. In fact, the beach is a very good location to enjoy kite surfing. This fun sport is a combination of windsurfing, skateboarding, and paragliding. It’s certainly a to delight the adventure-seeker.
 

iv. Sinquerim Beach

 Featuring a host of water-based adventurous activities, the Sinquerim Beach. The sandy shoreline of invites you to enjoy a thrilling time. One of the most interesting activities to do here is to go on a dolphin safari. During this you can spot dolphins playing about in the wide sea. Seeing these elegant creatures in their natural habitat is an experience in itself.
 

v. Anjuna Beach 

 A visit to the lively Anjuna Beach flea market definitely counts among the top things to do in Goa. This market comes alive every Wednesday. With a plethora of knick-knacks, souvenirs, junk jewellery, beachwear, shoes, bags, and whatnot. The icing on the cake is that you can buy all these and more at throw-away prices. When you have shopped, you can watch a mellow sundowner at Purple Martini. You can even head to Curlies or Thalassa  to watch a happening night unravel before you! 

 Strutting in the streets!

Here is the ultimate destination to film reels! Take shots for a well awaited aesthetic feed you were looking for! Unfurl your inner artist as you head here and find age-old bakeries and rejuvenate.

i. Buildings and Bars

 Every edifice here is an architectural masterpiece. The naturally grown floral creepers blossom on the vibrantly coloured walls. They make every spot photo-worthy. You will witness several professionals around here capturing the beauty of these streets. The locals here keenly narrate the history of the place and their ancestors. You can find several Irish cafes and steak houses around the corner. Well hidden amongst the streets is The Joseph’s bar. With effortless beauty, this bar settles in a rustic building. This is perhaps why it has been the most underrated Goan bar. The locals pass time over freshly brewed lagers and share anecdotes. This is the perfect place to try Kokam and Kaju Fenis and locally brewed meads. 
 

iv. Bakeries and Artisties  

 Panji is the home to Goa’s cultural heritage. It has the true essence of goan traditions. The bakeries here have a history of generations carrying their authentic recipes. One such bakery is Confeitaria 31, situated near Immaculate conception church. This hundred year old goan bakery is ran by two ladies. They greet every visitor pleasantly. They have successfully looked over this venture for decades. If you have wandered enough, next destination is a perfect place for an appetising meal. Grab a juicy beef burger or bacon with a side of eggs! Witness the art gallery which integrates some of works of powerful artists. Which make bold statements through their creations with regard to several social issues.
